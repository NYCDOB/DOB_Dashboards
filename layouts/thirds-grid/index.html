DOB DEVELOPMENT REPORT 2016
Permit Types by Community District (2016)
CD105 in Manhattan had the greatest overall number of permits issued and renewed and the greatest number of A2, and A3 job types citywide. While not in the top 10 Community Districts for overall number of permits issued and renewed, CD503 in Staten Island had he greatest number of NB job type permits citywide and CD407 had the greatest number of DM job type permits citywide.
CO & TCOs Issued on New Building Permits (2016)
In 2016, there were about 2000 Certificates of Occupancy (CO) and 3900 Temporary COs issued on New Building (NB) permits. Almost 40% of TCOs issued in 2016 were in Manhattan, but only 4% of COs. On average, an NB moved from initial permit to final CO in two years, whereas NBs with a TCO issued were closer to four years. The larger points on the map show properties with a longer time from permit to CO/TCO issuance.
Permits Issued (2000 - 2016)
The greatest year-to-year increase in total initial and renewed permits occurred from 2003 to 2004. During that time New Building permits increased by 20% and Demolitions increased by 27%. This upward surge in permitting activity continued through 2005, but was followed by a six year period of declining New Building and Demolition permits which both dropped almost 70%. However, since 2013, New Building and Demolition permits have again steadily increased by 40%. The overwhelming majority of permits issued since 2000 are for ALT 2 permits which are for minor alterations in which the use or occupancy of a facility does not change.
COs and TCOs Issued on NB/ALT1 Permits (2005 - 2016)
The greatest proportion of job types within each Borough was A2 with Manhattan having the greatest number overall (54% of A2 job types citywide). The greatest number of NB job types issued and renewed occurred in Queens (31% of NB job types citywide). DM job types represent the smallest proportion of job types within each Borough. Queens had the greatest number of DM job types issued and renewed (38% of DM job types citywide).
Demolition Permits Density (2016)
This density map shows concentrations of demolition permits issued in 2016. Darker shades of red indicate higher concentrations of demolition permits. These high concentration areas include midtown Manhattan, Bushwick and South Park Slope in Brooklyn, and Elmhurst and Astoria in Queens. The Community District with the greatest number of demolition permits was 407 in Queens. Willets Point, located in CD407, is highlighted on the map as an example of an area with clustered demolition permits issued (see zoomed-in map to the right).
Demolition Permits Cluster Example: Willets Point, Queens
Demolition permits were filed in July 2016 for 8 buildings in what is referred to as the "Iron Triangle" located just east of Citi Field in Willets Point, Queens. The demolition of the former auto repair shops began in late July to make room for a mixed-use redevelopment project.
